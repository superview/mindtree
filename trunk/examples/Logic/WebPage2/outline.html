A definition shall state only the essential attributes of the species.

A definition shall not be circular.

A definition shall be neither too broad nor too narrow

A definition shall not be expressed in ambiguous, obscure, or figurative language. If the defined word is vague, the definition shall be only as vague as the defined word.

A definition shall not be negative where it can be affirmative.

A definition shall match, as far as possible, the emotional tone of the term defined.

Basic Theorems

1. P(¬A) = 1 - P(A)

2. If A is contradictory, P(A) = 0

3. 0 <= P(A) <= 1

4. If A and B are truth-functionally equivalent, then P(A) = P(B).

5. P(A v B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A ^ B)

First demonstrate by truth table, the following:

a. A is truth-functionally equivalent to (A ^ B) v (A ^ ¬B)

b. B is truth-functionally equivalent to (A ^ B) v (¬A ^ B)

c. A v B is truth-functionally equivalent to ((A ^ ¬B) v (¬A ^ B)) v (A ^ B).

d. A ^ B and A ^ ¬B are mutually exclusive.

e. A ^ B and ¬A ^ B are mutually exclusive.

f. (A ^ ¬B) v (¬A ^ B) and A ^ B are mutually exclusive.

g. A ^ ¬B and ¬A ^ B are mutually exclusive.

By item (a) and Thm4, P(A) = P((A ^ B) v (A ^ ¬B)), whence by item (d) and AX3 it follows that:

h. P(A) = P(A ^ B) + P(A ^ ¬B)

Similarly, by item (b) and Thm4, P(B) = P((A ^ B) v (¬A ^ B)), whence by item (e) and AX3 we get:

i. P(B) = P(A ^ B) + P(¬A ^ B)

Moreover,

j. P(A v B) = P(A ^ ¬B) + P(¬A ^ B) + P(A ^ B)

Now by adding the equations in items(h) and (i), we note that:

k. P(A) + P(B) = P(A ^ ¬B) + P(¬A ^ B) + 2 P(A ^ B)

That is,

l. P(A v B) - (P(A) + P(B)) = -P(A ^ B)

Adding P(A) + P(B) to both sides of this equation yields P(A v B) = P(A) + P(b) - P(A ^ B), which was to be proved.

6. P(A ^ B) <= P(A)

7. P(A v B) >= P(A) and P(A v B) >= P(B)

8. If P(A) = P(B) = 0, then P(A v B) = 0

9. If P(A) = 1, then P(A ^ B) = P(B)

10. If A is a truth-functional consequence of B, then P(A ^ B) = P(B)

Pairwise Mutually Exclusive Series

Important Lemma: C11. If A1, A2, ..., An is a pairwise mutually exclusive and exhaustive series, then P(B) = P(A1 ^ B) + P(A2 ^ B) + ... + P(An ^ B).

Let A1, A2, ..., An be a pairwise mutually exclusive and exhaustive series. Then P(A1 v A2 v ... v An) = 1, and so, by Thm 9.

a. P((A 1 v A2 v ... v An) ^ B) = P(B)

b. P(B) = P((A1 ^ B) v (A2 ^ B) v ... v (An ^ B))

c. The series (A1 ^ B), (A2 ^ B), ..., (An ^ B) is pairwise mutually exclusive.

d. Now from (c), it follows that for each i such that 1 <= i < n, P(((A1 ^ B) v (A2 ^ B) v ... v (Ai ^ B)) ^ (Ai+1 ^ B)) = 0

e. From Thm 5 we obtain: P((A1 ^ B) v (A2 ^ B) v (A3 ^ B)) = P((A1 ^ B) v (A2 ^ B)) + P(A3 ^ B) - P(((A1 ^ B) v (A2 ^ B)) ^ (A3 ^ B))

f. Which reduces by d' to: P((A1 ^ B) v (A2 ^ B) v (A3 ^ B)) = P((A1 ^ B) v (A3 ^ B)) + P(A3 ^ B)

g. Now, again by Thm 5: P((A1 ^ B) v (A2 ^ B)) = P(A1 ^ B) + P(A2 ^ B) - P((A1 ^ b) ^ (A2 ^ B))

h. Which reduces by d'' to: P((A1 ^ B) v (A2 ^ b)) = P(A1 ^ b) + P(A2 ^ B)

i. Combining f and h, we get: P((A1 ^ B) v (A2 ^ B) v (A3 ^ B)) = P(A1 ^ B) + P(A2 ^ B) + P(A3 ^ B)

Which, by b', gives: P(B) = P(A1 ^ B) + P(A1 ^ B) + P(A3 ^ B)

Which proves the theorm for n=3.

General Form C12: If A1, A2, ..., An is a pairwise mutually exclusive and exhaustive series and Ai is some member of this series, then P(Ai|B) = (P(Ai) * P(B|Ai)) / (P(A1)*P(B|A1) + P(A2)*P(B|A2) + ... + P(An) * P(B|An))

Assume A1, A2, ..., An is a pairwise mutually exclusive and exhaustive series. Now by Thm C10 we know that

a. P(Ai|B) = (P(Ai) * P(B|Ai)) / P(B)

b. and by Thm C11 we have P(B) = P(A1 ^ B) + P(A2 ^ B) + ... + P(An ^ B)

c. Applying Thm C6 to formula (b) we get: P(B) = P(A1) * P(B | A1) + P(A2) * P(B | A2) + ... + P(An) * P(B | An)

Then applying formula (c) to formula (a) proves the theorem
